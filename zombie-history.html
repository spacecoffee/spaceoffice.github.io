Zombies, what are they?

Zombies have been referred to by different names throughout the ages. They come in many different forms. Their origins are unknown, but there are multiple inferences. Zombies are essentially a human corpse reanimated. Often referred to as undead, different variations are either flesh and brain eating beings.
Experts say “Zombies don't think for themselves. Whether they are consumed by rage, a mind-controlling disease or magic spell, or are simply too dead to reason anymore, a proper zombie cannot think for itself. This is what makes them so scary - there is no reasoning with them, no cure for their infection, they don't get tired, and they don't stop coming at you, no matter what.”

Voodoo Zombie 1932

  • Turns by drinking a special concoction
  • Mindless minion
  • No free will
  • Living person

Living Dead 1968

  • Reanimated corpse
  • Can only be killed by decapitation
  • Strong but slow
  • Feels no pain

Viral Zombie 1996

  • Created in a top-secret experiment
  • Bite and claws
  • Rotting flesh

Rabid Zombie 2002

  • Blood-eyed
  • Vomits blood
  • Fast runner

Bitter 2010

  • Can survive being cut in half
  • Impaired movement
  • Recognizes walkers by smell
  • The only way to kill it is smash its head or starve it to death

Z Zombies 2013

  • Behave like a destructive force of nature
  • Attracted by noise
  • Needs to be burnt down to die
  • Emits distinctive chattering sound

Dying Light Volatile 2015

  • Hunts only at night
  • Can jump and climb
  • Extremely strong and agile
  • Vulnerable to UV light
