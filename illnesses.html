Zombie Infections VS. Other Similar Infections

Just like how whatever disease manages to turn regular people into mindless, hungry, unthinking monsters, there are a few real life diseases that can cause similar symptoms.
The most common viral disease that gets compared to zombieism is the rabies virus, and for good reason. Rabies spreads via saliva entering a wound either through a bite or a scratch, and the first few signs of rabies mirror flu-like symptoms. Some similarities that are shared between rabies and getting bitten by a zombie are:

  • Fever & flu-like symptoms
  • Strange and abnormal behavior
  • The need to attack other people nearby increase the fatality rate


There is another instance of zombieism in nature, although it is caused by parasitic fungi instead of viral infection. Ophiocordyceps spores infect the brains of certain species of ants to gain control of their bodies and spread their spores to other ants in the process. The fungus fires certain chemicals into the nervous system of the ant, which makes them move any direction that would get them to spread further.

Some similarities that are shared between Ophiocordyceps and zombieism are:

  • Not being able to think for yourself
  • Unwillingly moving around
  • Coming into contact with others to try and spread the disease
